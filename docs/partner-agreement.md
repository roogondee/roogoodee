# Partnership Agreement Checklist — roogondee.com × W Medical Hospital

**Use this to structure the conversation, not as a legal document.** Get a
Thai lawyer to review any actual contract before signing.

---

## 1. Commercial model (most critical — decide first)

### Who pays for ad spend?

- [ ] Option A: roogondee fronts the ad spend; W Medical pays per outcome
- [ ] Option B: W Medical pays ad spend directly; roogondee runs the campaigns
- [ ] Option C: split 50/50 (simple but less aligned incentives)

**Recommendation:** Option A. roogondee's skin-in-the-game keeps ad spend
efficient.

### What's the payable outcome?

Pick one (or a tiered combination):

- [ ] **Per lead delivered** (฿100-200) — highest risk for W Medical (may get bad leads)
- [ ] **Per voucher redeemed at hospital** (฿300-500) — middle ground, aligned with visit
- [ ] **Per customer** (฿3,000-5,000 depending on service) — lowest risk for W Medical, highest upside for roogondee

**Recommendation for start:** per-voucher-redeemed. This incents quality
(W Medical won't pay for fake/low-intent leads) while being measurable.

### Tier pricing?

Different prices for different services, since LTV differs drastically:

- GLP-1 customer LTV: ฿30,000-150,000 → per-customer price ฿3,000-7,500 reasonable
- CKD: ฿10,000-50,000 → ฿1,000-2,500
- STD/PrEP: ฿5,000-30,000 → ฿500-1,500 (but volume likely higher)

Negotiation range: **10-15% of expected first-year LTV** per customer.

## 2. SLA — response time commitments

What happens if W Medical doesn't contact a lead quickly?

- [ ] Urgent tier (`<72h` STD) — must contact within **30 min**
- [ ] Hot tier — within **1 hour** (GLP-1) or **4 hours** (CKD)
- [ ] Warm tier — within **24 hours**
- [ ] Cold tier — auto-nurture, no SLA required

**If SLA missed:** define the consequence. Options:
- Credit-back: that lead doesn't count toward billing
- Escalation to manager: third miss = meeting to review process
- Hard cap: if >10% of leads miss SLA for 2 weeks, contract review

---

## 3. Data ownership + usage rights

This clause protects future revenue for roogondee.com.

- [ ] roogondee.com **retains ownership** of the lead database
- [ ] W Medical has **usage right** for the leads they contacted (not ownership)
- [ ] W Medical cannot:
  - [ ] Transfer lead data to another partner
  - [ ] Market for services outside the 3 agreed services without consent
  - [ ] Retain contact info after contract termination (must delete within 30 days)
- [ ] roogondee.com retains right to:
  - [ ] Re-market to the same leads for *unrelated* services (e.g., general health content)
  - [ ] Re-market to users whose voucher expired unused
  - [ ] Use aggregated/anonymized data for analytics + case studies

**PDPA note:** our consent form says "ยินยอมให้ รู้ก่อนดี(รู้งี้) และ
W Medical Hospital เก็บและใช้ข้อมูล" — this needs to match the contract
scope. If W Medical gets unlimited use, the consent text should reflect
that.

---

## 4. Exclusivity

- [ ] **Exclusive** — roogondee.com sends only to W Medical (simpler, higher commission)
- [ ] **Non-exclusive** — roogondee may send to multiple hospitals (flexibility, lower commission per lead)

**Recommendation:** exclusive for first 6 months, then review.

### Geographic scope

- [ ] Samut Sakhon + nearby provinces (current plan)
- [ ] Other provinces → separate agreement with other hospitals later

---

## 5. Medical liability

- [ ] W Medical is solely responsible for medical decisions (treatment plans, prescriptions, results)
- [ ] roogondee.com provides screening tools only; all copy reviewed by W Medical's medical advisor before publish
- [ ] If a customer complains about medical outcome:
  - Contact W Medical directly
  - roogondee has no liability for medical advice
- [ ] GLP-1 specifically: W Medical handles informed-consent process, roogondee does not promise specific weight loss outcomes

---

## 6. Term + termination

- [ ] Initial term: **3 months** (short enough to adjust if unit economics don't work)
- [ ] Auto-renewal: 12-month terms after initial if both perform
- [ ] Either side can terminate with **30 days notice**
- [ ] On termination:
  - [ ] W Medical honors all unexpired vouchers (obligation to customer)
  - [ ] roogondee stops directing traffic within 48 hours
  - [ ] W Medical deletes lead data within 30 days (PDPA requirement)

---

## 7. Payment terms

- [ ] Billing cycle: **weekly invoice** based on voucher redemption report
- [ ] Payment terms: Net 15 from invoice
- [ ] Minimum monthly commitment: none in first quarter; revisit after

---

## 8. Reporting + transparency

- [ ] roogondee provides W Medical **read-only access** to `/admin/analytics` (create them a user with role=manager or a limited role)
- [ ] Monthly performance review meeting (30 min)
- [ ] W Medical provides customer-conversion data back so roogondee can compute LTV

---

## Open questions (ask W Medical)

1. What's the realistic capacity for new patients/month across the 3 services?
2. Is there a hospital-side SLA commitment? Staffing on weekends?
3. Do they have own CRM that needs integration (HubSpot/Pipedrive) — or will they work off our admin + LINE?
4. Any brand/copy approval process needed? (medical claims, specifically GLP-1 "ลดน้ำหนักถาวร" language)
5. Prescription process for GLP-1 — does customer need prior lab work, or can W Medical prescribe same-day?
6. PEP supply availability — can they start within 72 hours for urgent STD cases?

---

## Red flags to watch for during negotiation

- W Medical wants ownership of leads before payment — push back
- W Medical insists on pay-per-lead with no quality guarantee on our side — reject; pay-per-voucher-redeemed is the fair middle
- W Medical unwilling to commit to SLA — signal that their capacity is thin
- Refusal to provide conversion data back — blocks our learning and pricing future contracts
